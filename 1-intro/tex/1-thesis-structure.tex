\section{Thesis Structure}

This thesis is structured as follows: 
Chapter 2 establishes the theoretical and practical foundations of robot localisation for vineyard manipulation tasks. 
Chapter 3 presents the development and evaluation of a multi-sensor SLAM system for initial mapping and coarse localisation within vineyard environments. 
Chapter 4 describes the precise-alignment methodology, wherein robot pose estimates are refined through registration with pre-existing 3D models using stereo depth imaging, dense LiDAR, and Iterative Closest Point (ICP) algorithms. 
Chapter 5 concludes with a synthesis of key findings and recommendations for future research.
