            between mean (39.6 m) and median (13.1 m) for max_pos_err illustrates the
            importance of reporting full statistics rather than scalar summaries.

            failure) is arguably a larger practical concern than a uniformly elevated error,
            because it is unpredictable at deployment time. The refined parameters do not
            eliminate the fundamental instability of scan-matching-only odometry in structured
            environments; they delay its onset.
          - Discuss: whether the catastrophic-failure run(s) can be linked to environmental
            factors (GNSS coverage, route geometry) is unknown without per-run trajectory
            visualisation — flag this as a limitation.

        \subsubsection{Proposed Method — Generalisation and Consistency}
          - Mean error_pct on Evaluation Dataset (~0.0166%) is directly comparable to
            the parameterisation dataset range (0.0153%–0.0501%) and falls within it.
          - Critically: std across 5 runs is extremely small (0.000354%), indicating
            near-deterministic behaviour. The GNSS global constraint appears to stabilise
            the trajectory to the point where the non-determinism of the underlying
            odometry has negligible effect on the final output.
          - GASP (mean 0.0508, std 0.0081) is comparable to parameterisation dataset
            values (0.0274–0.0679), confirming that the GNSS architecture generalises
            without degradation.
          - This provides strong evidence against overfitting: the proposed method's
            improvement is attributable to architectural design (absolute positional
            observability via GNSS) rather than incidental tuning to training trajectories.

        \subsubsection{Summary of Generalisation Assessment}
          - 1–2 sentences: all three methods exhibit performance on the Evaluation Dataset
            that is consistent with the parameterisation datasets, supporting the
            generalisation of the results. The key differentiator is not average accuracy
            but robustness: the proposed method is consistent across runs while the refined
            GLIM retains the risk of catastrophic failure.

5. KEY CONTENT POINTS

   METHODOLOGY:
   [1] Five parameterisation datasets span two environments: one apple orchard (Dataset 1)
       and one vineyard (Datasets 0, 2, 3, 4). This environmental diversity was deliberate
       to avoid over-specialisation of the parameterisation.
   [2] Dataset 2 is distinct because: it starts from the opposite end of the vineyard
       row compared to the other vineyard datasets, the altitude decreases (not increases)
       on the forward traversal, and there is no return pass — the route is one-directional.
   [3] Dataset 1 (apple orchard) has significantly more elevation variation (dips and bumps)
       than the vineyard datasets, making it more challenging for the altitude axis.
   [4] Evaluation Dataset: same vineyard as Datasets 0, 2, 3, 4; distinct route; same
       altitude profile as Datasets 0, 1, 3, 4 (increase then decrease).
   [5] Evaluation Dataset was withheld from all parameterisation stages. The purpose is to
       test whether performance improvements are transferrable or route-specific.
   [6] Five runs per method on the Evaluation Dataset: justified by non-determinism in SLAM
       (due to parallelism and floating-point ordering). Single runs risk reporting an
       unrepresentative outlier. Aggregate statistics (mean, std, median, min, max) are
       reported.

   RESULTS:
   [7] Baseline GLIM Evaluation Dataset: mean error_pct = 1.96% (std 0.45%); mean RMSE 3D
       = 17.48 m; mean ATE = 13.39 m. Trajectory length ~682 m.
   [8] Refined GLIM Evaluation Dataset: mean error_pct = 0.62% (std 0.11%); but max_pos_err
       mean = 39.6 m with std = 61.0 m and max = 148.6 m (catastrophic failure in ≥1 run).
       Median max_pos_err = 13.1 m — far more representative of typical performance.
   [9] Proposed Method Evaluation Dataset: mean error_pct = 0.0166% (std 0.000354%);
       mean RMSE 3D = 0.146 m; mean ATE = 0.114 m; GASP mean = 0.0508 (std 0.0081).
       Highly consistent across all five runs.

   DISCUSSION:
   [10] Performance hierarchy (baseline < refined < proposed) is preserved on unseen data —
        evidence that results are not artefacts of the training data.
   [11] Baseline shows expected variability; no overfitting concern since it has no tuning.
   [12] Refined GLIM: bimodal — mean error_pct comparable to parameterisation datasets, but
        at least one run hit catastrophic failure (max_pos_err = 148.6 m). Demonstrates that
        parameter optimisation does not eliminate the fundamental scan-matching instability.
   [13] The discrepancy between mean (39.6 m) and median (13.1 m) for refined GLIM
        max_pos_err on the Evaluation Dataset underscores the importance of reporting full
        run statistics rather than scalar means.
   [14] Proposed method: near-zero variance across runs confirms that GNSS integration
        effectively suppresses the non-deterministic component of the odometry pipeline.
        This is a practically important property — a deploying operator can rely on
        consistent performance rather than treating any given run as a lottery.
   [15] The proposed method's Evaluation Dataset error_pct (0.0166%) is consistent with
        and falls within the parameterisation dataset range (0.0153%–0.0501%), providing
        strong evidence against overfitting.

10. STYLE NOTES

    - Tone: factual and restrained throughout. Results prose should be purely descriptive;
      interpretive claims belong in the discussion.
    - In the methodology subsection, use plain declarative sentences for dataset descriptions.
      Avoid superlatives. Do not editorialize (e.g., "challenging" is acceptable; "very
      challenging" is not unless quantitatively grounded).
    - In the discussion, maintain the same analytical register as the existing discussion
      subsections. Compare numbers directly. Where acknowledging limitations (e.g., not
      knowing which run was the catastrophic failure), use hedged language: "suggest",
      "indicate", "may be attributable to".
    - Terminology: always "Evaluation Dataset" (capitalised) when referring to the held-out
      set. "Parameterisation datasets" (lower-case) for Datasets 0–4 collectively.
      Do not use "test set" or "training set" — this is not a machine learning context.
    - "Non-deterministic" (not "random") when describing run variability.
    - "Catastrophic failure" is acceptable terminology — it is already used in the existing
      discussion (line 17: "catastrophic and irrecoverable failure").
    - Metric names: ATE, RPE, GASP, RMSE — all in caps without expansion after first use.
    - Do not introduce new acronyms.
    - Tense: past tense throughout ("was evaluated", "exhibited", "demonstrated").
    - UK English: "optimisation", "generalisation", "behaviour", "neighbourhood".
